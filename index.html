Abdurehim Ötkür

One of the most influential Uyghur poets, novelists and translators in late modern literature.

Some of Abdurehim Ötkür's literatures published in Uyghur between early 1980s and 1990s. The cover of the book in the middle is Abdurehim Ötkür.

Here's a time line of Abdurehim Ötkür's Life:

  • May 1,1923 - Born in Kumul, Xinjiang
  • 1940 - Published his poems.
    The poems "Tarim Boyliri" (Along Tarim) and "Yürek Mungliri" (Melodies of the heart) were very popular.
  • 1942 - Graduated from Xinjiang University.
  • 1949 - After graduated, he then worked as the editor of the newspaper "Altay Geziti"(Altay Newspaper) till 1949.
  • 1950 - Worked as an interpreter in various governmental offices till 1980—as he knew Uyghur, Chinese, Russian and English all well.
  • 1980 - Ötkür worked as a scholar in the Institute of Literature Studies of the Academy of Philosophy and Social Sciences, Xinjiang.
  • 1980 - Published: Qeshqer Kéchisi (Night in Kashgar) in Ürümchi in Xinjiang People's Press.
  • 1985 - Published: Novel Iz (Track) in Ürümchi in Xinjiang People's Press.
  • 1985 - Published: Ömür Menzilliri (Destiny of Life) in Ürümchi in Xinjiang Youths Press.
  • 1988 - Was appointed as a Vice Chairman of the Society of Kutadgu Bilig Studies.
  • 1990 - Published his novel trilogy: Oyghanghan Zimin (Awakened Land).
  • Oct 5,1995 - Died at the age of 72 in Urumqi, Xinjiang.
On Mar. 20, 2019, Harvard president Lawrence Bacow delivered a speech at Peking University in China with the theme “The Pursuit of truth and the Mission of the University”. He concluded with a verse from a poem written by Abdurehim Ötkür:

Along life’s road I have always sought truth,
In the search for verity, thought was always my guide.
My heart yearned without end for a chance of expression,
And longed to find words of meaning and grace.
Come, my friends, let our dialogue joyfully begin.
